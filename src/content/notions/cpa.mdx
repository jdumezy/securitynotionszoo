CPA represents the standard baseline for passive security in encryption schemes.
All FHE schemes usable in practice (BFV, BGV, CKKS, and TFHE) natively achieve CPA security.

**The CPA Game:**
In the CPA game, the adversary has access to an encryption oracle (which is strictly necessary only in the private-key setting, as public-key adversaries can encrypt on their own) and a challenge oracle.
*   The challenge oracle can only be invoked once with two distinct messages ($m_0, m_1$), returning an encryption of $m_b$ based on a secret bit $b$ chosen by the challenger.
*   When evaluating homomorphic schemes under the CPA game, the adversary performs homomorphic evaluations entirely on their own. The adversary wins if they can guess the secret bit $b$.
