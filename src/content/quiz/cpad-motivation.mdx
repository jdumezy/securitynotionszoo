explanation: "The CPAD decryption oracle only accepts *legitimate* ciphertexts, i.e. ciphertexts derived from prior encryption and evaluation queries on adversary-chosen plaintexts. For a correct FHE scheme, $\\mathsf{Dec}(c)$ equals the plaintext the adversary already knows, so the oracle is useless and CPAD collapses to CPA. For approximate FHE (CKKS), $\\mathsf{Dec}(c)$ differs slightly from the exact plaintext, and this difference encodes the LWE noise. Li and Micciancio (2021) showed this is enough to recover the secret key, proving CPAD is strictly stronger than CPA in the approximate regime."
