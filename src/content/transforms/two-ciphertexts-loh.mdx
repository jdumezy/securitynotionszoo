A SNARK-free vCCA construction $P^{(2)}$ for the additively homomorphic Paillier cryptosystem, proved secure by Checri, Clet, Renard, and Sirdey (ePrint 2025/894). The encryption of a message $m$ is a pair of Paillier ciphertexts $(\mathsf{Enc}(m), \mathsf{Enc}(\alpha \cdot m))$ under the same key material for a secret random $\alpha$, together with an image-verification algorithm that decrypts both halves and checks the linear relation; an inconsistent pair decrypts to $\bot$ and is rejected by the vCCA decryption oracle. The "two-ciphertexts" template itself goes back to Bitansky, Chiesa, Ishai, Ostrovsky, and Paneth (TCC 2013) and Gennaro, Gentry, Parno, and Raykova (Eurocrypt 2013) in the context of succinct arguments of knowledge.

The security proof relies on the **Linear-Only Homomorphism (LOH)** assumption of Bitansky et al.: any adversary that produces a valid pair of ciphertexts passing the linear consistency check must know a linear combination of previously seen pairs that explains the output, and an extractor recovers that combination from the adversary's code and auxiliary input. LOH is a non-falsifiable knowledge-type assumption in the same family as Knowledge-of-Exponent, which lets the construction bypass SNARK machinery at the cost of a stronger algebraic assumption; it only applies to linearly homomorphic schemes such as Paillier and does not extend to full leveled FHE.
