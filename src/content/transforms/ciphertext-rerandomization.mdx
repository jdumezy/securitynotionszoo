If a statistically correct FHE scheme has **re-randomizable ciphertexts** and fresh ciphertexts are perfectly correct, then re-randomizing each input ciphertext before evaluation yields **ACER correctness** (adversarial correctness with evaluation re-randomization). This removes the ability of a sCPAD adversary to exploit their control over encryption randomness to force correlated failures.

The transform is essentially a hygiene rule: "before you evaluate, re-randomize". It converts the guarantees you had as a plain CPA-secure scheme with malleable fresh ciphertexts into the stronger sCPAD regime without changing the underlying scheme.
