The Naor-Yung paradigm lifts an IND-CPA public-key encryption scheme to IND-CCA1 by publishing **two independent encryptions** of the same message under two separate public keys, together with a non-interactive zero-knowledge proof of plaintext equality.

The proof forces the adversary to choose ciphertexts whose plaintexts it already knows, which lets the simulator answer any pre-challenge decryption query by decrypting either copy. A simulation-sound NIZK is required: the adversary must not be able to produce new valid proofs for false statements even after seeing simulated proofs.

Variants extend the paradigm to full CCA2 (e.g., Sahai 1999) and underpin later constructions such as the vCCA framework for FHE.
