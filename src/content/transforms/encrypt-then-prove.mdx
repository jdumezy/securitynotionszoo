The public-key publicly verifiable route to vCCAD proposed by Brzuska, Canard, Fontaine, Phan, Pointcheval, Renard and Sirdey (CIC 2025). Start from an **sCPAD-secure FHE scheme** (strong CPAD: the adversary controls encryption randomness, noise leakage is handled). Attach each fresh ciphertext to a **simulation-extractable zk-SNARK** for the well-formedness language (proving it is an honest encryption of some message under some randomness), and attach each evaluated ciphertext to a **straightline-extractable zk-SNARK** for correct homomorphic derivation from previously verified inputs.

Together the two SNARKs provide the vCCAD witness extractor used to implement the semantic-filter decryption oracle, and because both are publicly verifiable the construction works in the public-key setting with anyone as verifier. Replacing CPA with sCPAD at the base layer is what closes the approximate-FHE noise channel that vanilla vCCA would otherwise leave open, and gives the first compact publicly verifiable construction that reaches vCCAD in the general (approximate) regime.
