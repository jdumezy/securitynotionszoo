The Fujisaki-Okamoto (FO) transform upgrades any IND-CPA public-key encryption scheme (and even any one-way PKE, in some variants) into an IND-CCA2 scheme, provably secure in the **random oracle model**.

The idea: derandomize encryption by using the hash of the message and some randomness as the encryption randomness, then bind the message to a key-encapsulation wrapper checked via a re-encryption test at decryption time. Any ciphertext that would decrypt "differently" from how an honest sender would have produced it is rejected as invalid.

The FO transform and its modern variants are the backbone of most post-quantum KEMs, including the NIST-standardized lattice-based schemes Kyber and NTRU.
